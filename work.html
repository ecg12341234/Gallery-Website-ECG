Speculative Concepts

Unsolicited ideas for Shanghai brands we admire.

So Acai

Acai bowl café / health & wellness

The Opportunity

So Acai has beautiful product photography and a strong visual identity. They're positioned as premium and health-focused — but their social presence feels corporate. There's room to lean into lifestyle storytelling, community, and the mindful living angle.

Our Approach
  • Angle 1: From "product showcase" to "morning ritual" — shift content to celebrate the ritual of ordering and enjoying acai, not just the bowl itself.
  • Angle 2: From "health facts" to "Gen Z wellness" — speak to real wellness (mental health, sustainability, authenticity) instead of just nutrition.
  • Angle 3: From "solo product" to "community story" — feature customers, collaborations, behind-the-scenes team stories.
Sample Direction
  • "First sip moment" series — close-up, candid photos of customers' first bite with caption: "the pause before paradise"
  • "Sustainable cups" campaign — show the reusable bowl initiative with user-generated content from repeat customers

Max Match Vintage

Vintage fashion resale / sustainability

The Opportunity

Max Match has a unique product — curated vintage with personality. But their messaging is purely transactional. Gen Z cares about sustainability, authenticity, and storytelling. There's huge potential to position vintage as the cooler, more sustainable alternative to fast fashion.

Our Approach
  • Angle 1: From "items for sale" to "story of each piece" — every post highlights the history, era, and who might have worn it.
  • Angle 2: From "discount fashion" to "investment pieces" — frame vintage as smarter, cooler, more sustainable than buying new.
  • Angle 3: From "brand as curator" to "community as curator" — feature styling tips from followers, create #MaxMatchOutfits user-generated content.
Sample Direction
  • "Timeline series" — show the same vintage jacket styled 3 ways across decades (90s, 00s, today)
  • "Sustainability wins" — calculate CO2 saved per vintage purchase vs. new fast fashion equivalent
